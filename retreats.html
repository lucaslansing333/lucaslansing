II.

Private Retreats

Three to five days, designed and held for one client, one couple, one family, or a small private group. Conducted by Lucas Lansing and Callie Lansing.

What a retreat is

A designed pause.

A private retreat is a designed pause. Three to five days held entirely around a single client or household, in a setting chosen for its quiet, with the rhythms of cleansing, movement, nourishment, bodywork, and contemplative practice woven across each day with intention.

Most retreats are four days, Thursday through Sunday. Length, location, and design are calibrated to the client and the season they are in.

Who retreats are for

Held around a single household.

Couples seeking a few days of unhurried presence with each other before the next chapter. Founders and executives who need to step out of the operating tempo of their lives long enough for the nervous system to actually exhale. Families gathering — sometimes across generations — who want time together that is structured around something deeper than logistics. Individuals at thresholds: between roles, between decisions, between seasons of a life.

The shape of the retreat is determined by who is in it. A retreat for one founder is not a retreat for a couple, and a retreat for a family of seven is not a retreat for a couple. Each is designed accordingly.

The logic of a retreat day

The body is cleansed before it is moved, moved before it is fed, fed before it is worked, worked before it is rested, rested before it is met with stillness.

The rhythm of a typical day

Morning to evening, woven through.

Early morning
Cleansing
Ayurvedic practices to clear the body before the day begins — neti, oil pulling, tongue scraping. The system is tuned before it is asked to work.
Morning
Yoga & smoothie
A morning yoga practice followed by a liver-supportive smoothie. Movement before food; food in service of the practice.
Late morning
Strength & conditioning
Resistance training calibrated to the client, followed by a second nourishing smoothie. The body is taught to produce energy by spending it.
Midday
Lunch
Prepared to the client's protocol and dietary needs. The pace of the day shifts from active to integrative.
Afternoon
Bodywork & regulation
Massage, nervous system regulation, four-hands healing where appropriate, and contrast therapy in the sauna and cold plunge. The held part of the day.
Evening
Dinner & meditation
A quiet dinner. The day closes with an evening meditation, often Hong-Sau, and an early sleep.

The arc across the retreat

Each day opens what the previous one prepared.

Day one
Clears
The body sets down what it arrived carrying.
Day two
Deepens
The practice settles into the system. The mind begins to rest.
Day three
Opens
The deeper work, often unspoken, begins to surface.
Day four
Integrates
The client returns to ordinary life carrying something they did not arrive with.

Setting

Held in Tahoe, or where the work can land.

Retreats are held either at a Tahoe rental selected and prepared in advance for the client, or at the client's own residence — wherever the rhythm of the days can be most fully held.

Tahoe is a particular landscape. The lake itself, the alpine air, the proximity of stillness — these are not incidental to the work. For clients who travel here, the setting is part of the retreat. For clients hosting at their own residence, the practice is brought to them.

Who is present

A small, known team.

Retreats are conducted by Lucas Lansing and Callie Lansing together. Meals are prepared by us, or by a chef brought in for the retreat where the client prefers. Massage is given by Lucas; for retreats with multiple participants, additional licensed massage therapists are brought in.

The team is kept small and known. The client is not navigating a rotating cast of practitioners.

How retreats are designed

Built in advance, adjusted on the ground.

Each retreat is designed in advance. Following an initial conversation, we build a draft schedule and a draft protocol — meals, modalities, bodywork, integration time, any additional practitioners — and review it with the client before the retreat begins. Adjustments are made on the ground as the days unfold. No two retreats are identical.

How retreats begin

Each retreat begins with a conversation.

Retreats are scheduled a small number of times each year. If, after an initial conversation, the work is a fit on both sides, design and scheduling follow.

Investment is discussed in conversation.
